Executive Summary

OSINTNova (app.osintnova.com / osint.nova-saas.com) is a commercial OSINT platform offering a suite of intelligence-gathering tools, including People Intelligence, Phone Intelligence, Vehicle Intelligence, Discord Intelligence, and various social media scraping services.

The platform markets itself as an "AI-powered OSINT platform for advanced digital investigations and intelligence analysis." A direct copycat product, LittleNuan, has appeared on BuiltByBit, explicitly branding itself as a "cheaper version of OSINTNova."

Key Finding: OSINTNova aggregates and sells access to Personally Identifiable Information (PII) through its "PRO" tier, with no visible privacy policy, terms of service, or consent mechanisms. This raises significant legal and ethical concerns under multiple federal and state laws.

Alarming Questions That Must Be Asked

1. Is LittleNuan Actually the Same Service Under a Different Name?

The listing explicitly references OSINTNova by name. This raises the question: is LittleNuan a separate product, or is it the same underlying service being rebranded and resold? The fact that it's priced at $47.88 (a one-time fee) versus OSINTNova's subscription model suggests either a completely different product, a scam attempting to profit off OSINTNova's name, or the same service being resold with a different front-end.

Without source code access, this cannot be verified.

2. Where Does OSINTNova Source Its Data?

The platform offers access to Discord IDs, phone numbers, vehicle information, breach data, and behavioral profiles. None of these are public records in the traditional sense.

- Discord Intelligence: Discord does not make user data publicly available. This data must be scraped, which violates Discord's Terms of Service.
- Phone Intelligence: Selling access to phone records without consent is a federal crime under 18 U.S.C. § 1039.
- Breach Intelligence: If the platform is selling access to breach data, this constitutes trafficking in stolen information.
- Oracle/WhoAmI: These services compile comprehensive behavioral profiles from multiple data points — the kind of profiling typically associated with data brokers, not OSINT tools.

The platform does not disclose where its data comes from, how it's obtained, or whether it's legally sourced.

3. Are Users Unknowingly Breaking the Law by Using This Platform?

Many OSINT practitioners assume that using OSINT tools is always legal. However:

- Scraping Discord, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube violates those platforms' Terms of Service and may constitute unauthorized access under the CFAA (18 U.S.C. § 1030).
- Purchasing phone records may violate 18 U.S.C. § 1039.
- Using breach data may violate California Civil Code § 1724 and other state laws.

Users of OSINTNova may be exposing themselves to legal liability without realizing it.

4. Why Is There No Privacy Policy or Terms of Service?

As of this investigation, OSINTNova does not appear to have a visible privacy policy, Terms of Service, opt-out mechanisms, or data source disclosures.

This is a violation of CCPA, GDPR, and basic consumer protection standards.

5. Who Is Behind OSINTNova?

The platform's domain (osint.nova-saas.com) was registered recently and has a low Tranco rank, indicating few visitors. ScamAdviser rates it with a trust score of 66/100 — "medium to low risk."

However:
- The domain is very young
- The site is a website within another website
- There is no visible ownership or company information

This lack of transparency is itself a red flag.

6. Is OSINTNova Operating as an Unregistered Data Broker?

California law requires data brokers to register with the Attorney General. OSINTNova sells PII — phone numbers, vehicle information, behavioral profiles, and more. There is no evidence that OSINTNova is registered as a data broker in any jurisdiction.

This is a potential violation of California's data broker registry law.

7. What Happens to the Data You Submit?

The platform asks users to input names, phone numbers, Discord IDs, and other queries. Does OSINTNova store these queries? If so:
- Who has access?
- Is the data shared with third parties?
- Can users request deletion?
- Is the data being used to train models?

There are no answers to these questions on the platform.
