1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
=================================================

This document summarizes the findings of an open‑source investigation into OSINTNova (app.osintnova.com), a commercial OSINT platform offering a suite of intelligence services including breach intelligence, people/phone/vehicle lookup, crypto wallet tracking, dark web access, and social media scraping.

The investigation identified multiple red flags and potential violations of federal and state laws, including:
- 18 U.S.C. § 1030 (CFAA) — unauthorized access via scraping
- 18 U.S.C. § 1039 — sale of confidential phone records
- California Civil Code § 1724 — sale of data obtained via crime
- CCPA/CPRA — sale of PII without consent or opt‑out
- FTC Act § 5 — deceptive trade practices

Affiliated individuals were identified: "Goofisded" (Philippines, confirmed staff/head of OSINTNova) and "galacticpuffin" (Ryan, developer of NOVA OSINT platform, potential founder/developer). Geolocation through time patterns points to Philippines for Goofisded; others remain unidentified.

Actions taken: formal ticket submitted (withdrawn), public GitHub repository created (WinterGate-IC/OSINTNova), community notifications issued, and regulatory escalation is underway.

This document serves as the operational record of all findings, timelines, and next steps.

6. LEGAL CITATIONS (Full)
=================================================

6.1 Federal Statutes

- 18 U.S.C. § 1030 (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act)
  - Prohibits unauthorized access to protected computers.
  - Web scraping that violates ToS has been held to constitute unauthorized access in federal courts (e.g., hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn).
  - Applicable to OSINTNova's scraping of Discord, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, Steam.

- 18 U.S.C. § 1039 (Confidential Phone Records)
  - Knowingly selling or attempting to sell confidential phone records without customer authorization is a crime.
  - Penalty: up to 10 years imprisonment.
  - OSINTNova's Phone Intelligence PRO falls squarely within this prohibition.

- 18 U.S.C. § 1343 (Wire Fraud)
  - If the platform misrepresents its data sources or uses deceptive means to obtain data, wire fraud charges may apply.

6.2 State / Civil Laws

- California Civil Code § 1724
  - Unlawful to sell, or offer to sell, any data that was obtained or accessed pursuant to the commission of a crime.
  - Breach Intelligence PRO, if it sells data from a breach, violates this statute regardless of third‑party intermediary claims.

- California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA)
  - Consumers must be notified at collection, given the right to know, delete, and opt out of the sale of their personal information.
  - OSINTNova provides no prior notice, no opt‑out mechanism, and only a reactive blacklist (which does not satisfy CCPA).

- Federal Trade Commission Act § 5
  - Prohibits unfair or deceptive trade practices.
  - Selling sensitive personal data without transparency or consent is a deceptive practice, as seen in FTC v. Kochava and similar actions.

6.3 Platform Terms of Service Violations

- Discord, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, Steam all explicitly prohibit scraping.
- These ToS violations also expose the platform to civil claims and CFAA liability.
